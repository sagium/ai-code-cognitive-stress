Optimum is derived from your own history — the load level at which your closure ratio peaks and your off-hours follow-up is lowest. Anchored in Yerkes-Dodson's inverted-U (Yerkes & Dodson, 1908) and Csíkszentmihályi's flow channel (Csíkszentmihályi, 1990): performance and well-being follow an inverted-U with cognitive load, not a monotonic curve. Below = under-engaged; above = overload; sustained overload predicts burnout via cumulative allostatic load (McEwen, 1998).

Concurrent Open Decision Load (CODL)
3.2avg · peak 5.1 @ 13:30
+18% vs 30-day baseline
Technique
Count of Claude sessions in Ready state + 1.5× sessions in Blocked state, sampled every 30s and averaged over the work window.
Threshold rationale
Working memory caps at ~4 chunks. Sustained CODL > 4 exceeds typical WM bandwidth for concurrent decision-tracking. Cowan 2001; framework from Cummings & Mitchell 2008 on supervisory-control fan-out.
Caveat
Taskload (objective demand). Correlation with felt workload is moderate (r ≈ 0.4–0.6 across the HCI literature). Pair with NASA-TLX for diagnostic use.
Interruption Index
1.75per workhr · 14 events
+32% vs 30-day baseline
Technique
Weighted count of attention-pulling events per work hour. Weights: intra-Claude switch ×1, cross-session ×1.5, agent completion ×1.8, tool-block ×2, GitLab @-mention ×2.5.
Threshold rationale
Interrupted work completes faster but with measurably higher stress and effort. Mark, Gudith & Klocke 2008. External weighting reflects ~25% higher recovery cost vs. self-initiated switches (Mark, Gonzalez & Harris 2005). Residue accumulation: Leroy 2009.
Caveat
Source classification depends on local hooks & GitLab event API timing; ±2 minutes precision on external events.
Closure Deficit
0.55ratio
+11% vs 30-day baseline
Technique
Ratio of high-load time (CODL > p50) to closure-event count + idle-recovery minutes, integrated across the work window. 0 = perfect closure; 1 = no closure.
Threshold rationale
Burnout is predicted by demands exceeding recoverable resources, not by demands alone. Demerouti et al. 2001 (JD-R model). Closure of attentional loops is itself a resource (Leroy 2009); commits and merges are ground-truth closure markers.
Caveat
Implicit closure (long idle) is hard to distinguish from abandonment. Where the metric is ambiguous, only explicit closure (commit, merge, "done" markers) is counted — this biases toward over-reporting deficit.

Methodology & citations

This profile is generated from three data sources: Claude Code session transcripts (~/.claude/projects/<project>/*.jsonl), local git activity (commits, worktree mtimes, reflog), and GitLab events (MR notes, review requests, @-mentions, todos via glab api). All three are merged into a per-day timestamped event fabric and reduced into the four state classes (Driving / Waiting / Ready / Blocked) for each Claude session, and the three axes (CODL / Interruption Index / Closure Deficit) shown above. Work-hours windows are auto-detected from the 10th–90th percentiles of user-message timestamps per weekday, with optional override via the skill config file.

Research basis

  • Cowan 2001 Cowan, N. (2001). The magical number 4 in short-term memory: A reconsideration of mental storage capacity. Behavioral and Brain Sciences, 24(1), 87–114. Grounds the CODL ≥ 4 threshold.
  • Cummings & Mitchell 2008 Cummings, M. L. & Mitchell, P. J. (2008). Predicting controller capacity in supervisory control of multiple UAVs. IEEE Trans. Systems, Man, and Cybernetics — Part A, 38(2), 451–460. Fan-out / PSC framework.
  • Crandall & Cummings 2007 Crandall, J. W. & Cummings, M. L. (2007). Identifying predictive metrics for supervisory control of multiple robots. IEEE Trans. Robotics, 23(5), 942–951. Neglect-time / interaction-time vocabulary.
  • Csíkszentmihályi 1990 Csíkszentmihályi, M. (1990). Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience. Harper & Row. Flow channel — optimum-load concept, the band between boredom and anxiety.
  • Demerouti et al. 2001 Demerouti, E., Bakker, A. B., Nachreiner, F. & Schaufeli, W. B. (2001). The Job Demands–Resources Model of Burnout. Journal of Applied Psychology, 86(3), 499–512. Closure-Deficit framing — demands without resources predicts burnout.
  • Leroy 2009 Leroy, S. (2009). Why is it so hard to do my work? The challenge of attention residue when switching between work tasks. Organizational Behavior and Human Decision Processes, 109(2), 168–181. Attention-residue mechanism for unclosed switches.
  • Mark, Gudith & Klocke 2008 Mark, G., Gudith, D. & Klocke, U. (2008). The cost of interrupted work: more speed and stress. In Proc. CHI 2008. Interruption Index — productivity vs. stress tradeoff.
  • Mark, Gonzalez & Harris 2005 Mark, G., Gonzalez, V. M. & Harris, J. (2005). No task left behind? Examining the nature of fragmented work. In Proc. CHI 2005. ~23-minute return-to-task figure; external-interruption weighting.
  • Mark 2023 Mark, G. (2023). Attention Span: A Groundbreaking Way to Restore Balance, Happiness and Productivity. Hanover Square Press. Population baseline for fragmentation distribution.
  • Maslach & Jackson 1981 Maslach, C. & Jackson, S. E. (1981). The measurement of experienced burnout. Journal of Occupational Behavior, 2(2), 99–113. Maslach Burnout Inventory — validated diagnostic instrument referenced in disclaimer.
  • McEwen 1998 McEwen, B. S. (1998). Protective and damaging effects of stress mediators. New England Journal of Medicine, 338(3), 171–179. Allostatic-load model — within-day shape, not just totals.
  • Sheridan 1992 Sheridan, T. B. (1992). Telerobotics, Automation, and Human Supervisory Control. MIT Press. Founded the supervisory-control framework that the whole metric inherits from.
  • Sonnentag & Fritz 2007 Sonnentag, S. & Fritz, C. (2007). The Recovery Experience Questionnaire. Journal of Occupational Health Psychology, 12(3), 204–221. Closure as recovery resource.
  • Sonnentag, Binnewies & Mojza 2010 Sonnentag, S., Binnewies, C. & Mojza, E. J. (2010). Staying well and engaged when demands are high: the role of psychological detachment. Journal of Applied Psychology, 95(5), 965–976. Off-hours engagement signal.
  • Wickens 2008 Wickens, C. D. (2008). Multiple resources and mental workload. Human Factors, 50(3), 449–455. Justifies higher cost for cross-tool / cross-modality switches.
  • Hart & Staveland 1988 Hart, S. G. & Staveland, L. E. (1988). Development of NASA-TLX (Task Load Index). Advances in Psychology, 52, 139–183. Subjective workload instrument referenced in caveats.
  • Yerkes & Dodson 1908 Yerkes, R. M. & Dodson, J. D. (1908). The relation of strength of stimulus to rapidity of habit-formation. Journal of Comparative Neurology and Psychology, 18(5), 459–482. Inverted-U law — performance peaks at moderate arousal/load, declines at both extremes. Underpins the optimum-line target on the daily chart.

Honest caveats inherent to the technique. (1) Supervisory-control research was developed for UAV operators, not LLM users — the transfer is plausible but not yet peer-reviewed. (2) All metrics here are taskload, not workload; individual differences in subjective experience are large. (3) Personal baselines require a calibration period (default 14 days). (4) Cognitive-offloading and skill-atrophy concerns occasionally cited in popular discussions of LLM use (e.g. Kosmyna et al. 2024 MIT Media Lab preprint) are under-evidenced and are not load-bearing for this metric.
